Poor Marcello, outgamed by his father. In all seriousness, that scene for me holds the essence of this movie and of the character Marcello. In it, he is with his father, whom he hasn't seen in a long time. Even though he at first seems overly happy with his prescence, that quickly turns when he starts getting more attention from women than him. Marcello becomes bored and even borderline frustrated. Marcello, ultimatly is empty, devoid of any love, indulging himself in the superficial. The type of Character Fellini would later again explore in his film Il Casanova di Federico Fellini. And my controversial opinion might be that he did it even better with that one. The more absurdist approach and the even more outlandish persona fit the theme very well. Maybe it's because I watched Il Casanova di Federico Fellini first. Maybe it's because I was a bit too tired to fully grasp this one. All of which ofcourse doesn't take away the amazing performance by Marcello Mastroianni and the overall beauty to be found in La Dolce Vita.
