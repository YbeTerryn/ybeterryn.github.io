Because my local theatre fucked up my first screening and made the second half appear somewhat blurry, I did something I never done before and watched the same movie two times in theater. This to see whether my opinion would change if I wasn't preoccupied with finding out why the film felt so weird at times. (At a certain point I even wondered if my own eyesight had worsened, because no one reacted to the distorted visuals) Having seen it another time (in another theatre), I can conclude that I won't be needing new glasses and that I was too hard for Paul Thomas Andersons' epic action thriller. One Battle After Another really is as entertaining as they come. Even the second time, I wasn't bored for one minute. Leo's performance seemed even stronger and Sean Penn's creepiness was all over it, seriously, how good is he in this! A scene that I forgot to mention in my first review is the car chase, truly the icing on an already very tasty cake. The epic thriller set against the backdrop of America's migration politics was heading towards my first five star review of the 2025 releases. But one scene remained that took that away. One scene that even the second time rubbed me the wrong way. Maybe even more now because of my grown appreciation for the rest of the film. The fucking letter. I really think it is such a weak ending note. Like a last minute tear jerker thrown in to make it more Hollywoodesque. I just don't like it. It straight up bothers me. Why PTA? Why?
