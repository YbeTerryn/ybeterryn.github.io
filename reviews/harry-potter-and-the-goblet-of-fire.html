The Goblet of Fire is proof the Harry Potter franchise truly gets better along the way. It might not be fully on level with Alfonso Cuarón's entry, but it is definitely the second best in the series so far. Ralph Fiennes finally makes his appearance as the main villain of the franchise and what an introduction this man gets! The high point of the film, by a mile. Special credits also need to go to Emma Watson as the 'prom night' scene is the first time she excels from being an adorable child actor to a real interpreter of Hermione's emotions. Furthermore, the Goblet of Fire serves Harry his first real dilemma's, which give the character some much needed depth. Though not everything about his persona deserves praise. Harry is again mostly undergoing things (like in the first two films) than actually overcoming them. For the only wizard surviving the ol' Avada Kedavra (very stupid name for an unforgivable spell to be honest) he's again mostly left to the aid of others. I don't want to make a political statement with a family movie but still, Hermione is severely underappreciated in the wizarding world. If there was a march fot her acknowledgement, everyone should be in it. I think she has actually done the most work/solved the most pieces of the puzzle of any character in the Franchise. Even Dumbledore pales in comparison. Still her wit is mostly treated as a side gag, and her female body used as bait for the male characters. I get that there starting to become teenagers, but they really dialed the sexual innuendo level up to eleven in this one. The larger narrative, for the first time, seems more important than the episodic nature of the books, as the most immersive scenes are only prologues as to what's to come. The flaws that are plaguing the series from the beginning are still there, albeit less profound, than in the first two films. Plot Holes, or predictable outcomes are still very much present, but they have less impact in the plot. All in all a formidable entry in the series.
