Napoleon

Five and a half hours is a long time, even for an epic tale of this proportion. I am though very happy it exists because it gives Birth of a Nation a run for it's money in terms of technical innovation. Napoleon feels incredibly modern. The way themes and metaphors are intertwined, the close-ups, the shadow shots...

Napoleon, for me, is a very overrated historical figure, his strategic greatness is remembered more easily than his violent tyranny.
This being a through and through French film (Guillotines, Le Tricolore, repeated renditions of La Marseillaise and even exposed breast in 1927) it also seems to encompass this very one-sided view: Napoleon was born for greatness and was held back by his time and jealous peers.

Being originally planned as a six-part tale, one can only assume how his downfall would have been portrayed. Still, and this is again a very modern feeling quality of the film, there are cracks to be spotted in Napoleons larger than life persona. The way others constantly are frightened by him. The way Dieudonné portrays him. Even though the story and intertitles convince you otherwise, there seems to be something off when it comes to Bonaparte. Something that maybe would have been later explored.

In conclusion 'Napoleon' is rightfully remembered for it's technical marvel, even entertaining modern audiences, but the film doesn't really make for a conclusive biopic. After five and a half hours, it still feels like prologue. And even when it is filled with so much grandeur, that is a long, long time.
