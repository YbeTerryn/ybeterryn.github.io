Well, well, if it ain't the Scandinavians going at it again. Their sense of humor truly is unmatched. 'Den Sidste Viking' is flawed, surely, no doubt about it, but it is so charming at the same time. It almost feels like two films woven together. It goes from a family comedy to a brutally violent thriller in a matter of seconds and back. On top of that not every point the film tries to make comes across. Like the flashback effects are so ridiculous, it actually dismantles the impact. Yet it all is an enjoyable time at the cinema. Mads Mikkelsen's dead pan way of jumping out of the first opening he can find is one of the finest running gags in recent cinema. Besides it's exaggeration, the film does make some sincere points about generational trauma. Den Sidste Viking is Cinema well done. Will it enter the history books? Probably not. But that's one reason more to go and enjoy it's qualities now, while it is still talked about.
