The Master is filled with beautiful shots. Poetic in it's vulgarity. This character study deliberately underexplains the plot. Phillipe Seymour Hoffman, Joaquin Phoenix and Amy Adams each play at the highest acting level. Especially Hoffman's eerie charisma, which is as warm as it is disturbing. Paul Thomas Anderson dares to make a cult appear like a true family and even likeable, all the while still subtly critiquing it's structure. The Cause and it's delusions aren't even he main focus of the film. Carefully written, the Master is about three personalities, probably somewhat referring to the Id, Ego and Superego introduced by dear ol' Freud and their interference with each other. Although Freddie Quell might be a low-life, a loose dog running after his own animalistic impulses, the film suggests that he also might be man's freest specimen.
