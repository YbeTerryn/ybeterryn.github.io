After a long time, my girlfriend finally convinced me of watching the first Harry Potter film. I was mostly surprised at it's failing narrative features. Harry Potter's first adventure in Hogwarts doesn't really have the strongest plot. It's a poorly executed detective that throws in a last minute twist as an attempt to woo the audience. On top of that Harry's misery in the first half hour is so ridiculous that it becomes almost dumb. The acting leaves one wanting too, but since they are child actors and they are adorable together, Daniel Radcliffe, Emma Watson and Rubert Grint get away with it pretty easily. The Philosopher' Stone strongest feature is it's magical world building. Stepping onto the Hogwarts express is as magical for the audience as it must be for little Harry. The first half of the film is actually almost just a presentation of the world created, with event after event serving little to no addition to the plot or mystique, but just showcasing a wonderful world of wizards. Even if some of the early two thousand's usage of CGI has some laughable features it is no wonder that this film filled the minds of so many children with dreams and started a cult of fanatics.
