This has, apart from a character named Cesare, who is not even a somnambulist, nothing to do with the German silent classic of the same name. Still, as the opening titles suggest, it was an inspiration. In this, the supposed granddaughter of the original dr. Caligari is transferred into a mad scientist/dominatrix type character. The sexual themes are overt and all over the place, but the actual on screen explicitly of them is quite soft. So it isn't really a pure exploitation film, even if it tries hard to be one. It has a certain style to it, which makes the whole thing watchable. The weird, but very structured way in which the younger couple of psychiatrists deliver their lines is the absolute high point of the film. It is so choreographed and genius, that it doesn't make sense within the cheap feeling Dr. Caligari otherwise has. Not only the way in which it is delivered, but also the dialogues themselves are very poetic and jazzy. These positive points justify it's cult classic status. But don't be fooled since even it's surprising qualities, can't hide the fact that this is a cheap, often painfully lewd, midnight movie. So skip if that's not your cup of tea, you won't be missing out.
