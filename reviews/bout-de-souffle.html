À bout de souffle is like it's background music, jazz. Cinematic jazz. The whole thing feels so pure and so real. It could've only be produced in the way it was. As an artefact of one man's clouded vision, it's all over the place, but in the loveliest way possible. A revolutionary work in cinema that actually doesn't feel that out there, because of the widespread use of many of it's innovations. The two main actors are lovely to see interacting with each other. Only a few moments (mainly just before the third act) where the films pacing somewhat drags. The dialogue is so genius and genuinely funny at times. Richard Linklaters greatest achievement with his recent 'Nouvelle Vague' is providing cinema's all around with an excuse to represent screenings of this sixties epic by Jean-Luc Goddard, allowing me to explore it on the big screen.
