It is a jump in quality from the previous film for sure and it has a lot more entertaining value. Some of the action sequences are genuinely a thrill. Also Snape is back and Alan Rickman steals the show once again. Still the concluding chapter of Harry Potter's coming of age saga is underwhelming. There is so much character building and background lore in all these films and then much of it leads to... well, absolutely nothing. The Dumbledore family and their quarrels? What about James 'the Bully' Potter and the whole Severus Snape/Lilly Potter relationship? The films just skims all those topics. It's almost a paywall for the benefit of J.K. Rowling: Want to know more? Buy the books. It is logical that a film can never be as conclusive as a book, but I feel like the choice is between leaving something in and fully explaining it or leaving something out. The Deathly Hallows: Part 2 does everything half-assed. I also think that, while definitely stylish in execution the way Voldemort gets defeated is actually quite stupid. First of all I had to Google how/why Harry Potter survived the 'Avada Kedavra' since the movie completely underexplains it. But still, even when explained with the book version. It is all quite dumb. So you're gonna tell me Draco fucking Malfoy is the rightful owner of the Elder Wand, because he disarmed Dumbledore. Then Harry Potter disarmed the little blonde dipshit while the kid HAD NEVER IN IS HIS LIFE THOUCHED THAT THING and was even using another wand, but somehow the Elder wand ended up in such an allegiance with Harry that he couldn't be killed with it. I honestly can't be the only who thinks that is utter and complete bullshit, even for a story about people splitting there soul in pieces and love being a literal protection for evil. On top of that he is also the master of Death because he possesses the three Deathly Hallows. But there is a scene in which he purposely drops the resurrection stone. How does property ownership work at Hogwarts, what kind of fucked up capitalism system do they use? The franchise of Harry Potter is filled with little nice moments all throughout, but it just takes so many wrong turns too. Without the childhood nostalgia that it somehow created, it would rightfully be forgotten.
