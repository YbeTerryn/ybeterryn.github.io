With one of the best opening sequences in recent cinema, The Secret Agent sets the tone perfectly. A film that deliberately passes it's time with everything but the story. Purposefully unexplained, The Secret Agent entertains audiences with quirky characters and an competent lens. The run-up still feels slow though, like really slow, maybe at times just a tad too slow. But then comes the third act and the pay-off is simply incredible. Reminiscent of the Coen Brothers epic western: No Country for old Men, the shootout hides it's most important part without ever losing tension. Wagner Moura shines through all it's chaos and justifies his Palme d'or win at Cannes. As an evocation of 70's Brazil with fantasy-like interludes, the world feels both painfully real and absurdly surreal. On top of that Kleber Mendonça Filho hides it's funniest elements in the framing or editing. A daring project almost executed to perfection.
