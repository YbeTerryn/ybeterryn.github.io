With a structure that mirrors that of 'From Dusk Till Dawn', Sinners takes a long time before the horror is formally introduced. It is a time well spent though. As the audience is taken along the cotton fields of the early 20th century American South, we encounter some great characters and phenomenal music. The dialogues are sharp, the notes even sharper. Racial issues and the influence of the KKK are hidden throughout scenes. Sinners is an entertaining film done right. With an almost effortless blend of genres, though sometimes nearing complete randomness, Ryan Coogler makes the whole thing feels cohesive even when some plotpoints are underexplored.
