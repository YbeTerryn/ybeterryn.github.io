The second (I guess since finally giving in, I'm gonna have to watch my way true all of these now) installment of the Harry Potter Franchise does things better than the first, but does some things worse too. The plot is thicker than it's predecessor. Almost every scene moves the story forward and the mystery elements are more profound and better panned out. On the other hand it's more ridiculous than the first. Our little Harry isn't learning anything in his second year at Hogwarts, he is getting his ass saved by more Deus Ex Machina moments than an average Greek tragedy. For a movie that takes over two hours and a half to reach it's climax, every obstacle is easily overwon and like I said, not because of Potter getting better at magic. Actually, does he even do a spell that has any impact on the plot? For the most famous wizard in school, he's not wizarding much. Not only plot wise, but also in presentation, we get the same dichotomy. The mystery elements are darker and therefore stronger, but it is at the expense of the magical feel that was done so right in the Philosopher's stone. Chamber of Secrets did not make me love the adventures of Harry more but also didn't make me hate them either. Just more mediocracy.
