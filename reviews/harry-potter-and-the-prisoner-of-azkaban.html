I was surprised to see the sudden jump in quality from the two previous films, but when I saw that Alfonso Cuarón directed this one, it all made sense. The Prisoner of Azkaban improves on almost everything the franchise has to over. The mystery is deeper and for the first time actually surprising, even darker then the previous one. Harry Potter actually learns something in his third year, something that is even useful to the story. The plot seems deliberately underexplained at times, which makes the Harry Potter story feel like a larger arch for the first time. Cuarón did a great job at balancing the franchise story and the episodic year per year obstacles. Sure, it is not completely without flaws, most of them have plagued the series from the beginning. Either coming from the lack of originality from the source material or the way in which a movie can only grasp the surface of a book. The Prisoner Of Azkaban biggest mistake is reducing Draco Malfoy to a slapstick gag. Giving the blond troublemaker/bad boy some more depth is the only thing previous films did better. A shame since from all the youngsters Tom Felton is the most natural actor. The other child actors, now somewhat grown, still have difficulty realistically playing the bigger emotions, but they haven't lost their charm either. The adult cast is is on point though. Gary Oldman was such a joy to watch and David Thewlis was magnificent. Might be a premature statement, but I have a good feeling this one might become my favorite in the series.
