That is some biblical punishment for listening to techno! Sirāt is an incredibly good time at the movies. The beats and the world created is utterly transcending. I don't even like techno that much, but these sounds were amazing. It's a road trip film, with many moments of quiet travelling, but once the third act really explodes, you'll be holding your breath for minutes to come. Sirāt does the trick many modern movies try, changing the genre after the second act and it does it in contrary to other projects really well. Still it seems, and this is my only real critique, to offer up some of it's cohesiveness for shock-value. It might be me, but the metaphors are from the turning point on, not as clear anymore. Olivier Laxe definitely is telling something, I'm just not exactly clear what. There is the backdrop of the war, of military meddling, there are the ostracized punkers, there are themes of family, community and love. There is perseverance and desperateness. Like it's full of elements that are just blown up for a (admittingly nerve-wrecking) third act. Still the shockwave will keep your head busy for days to come.
