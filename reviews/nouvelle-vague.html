More than David Fincher's 'Mank' Nouvelle Vague sparks that time-machine magic. In terms of characters hanging out with each other, it is reminiscent of Linklater's work in 'Dazed and Confused', but by no means achieves the same feeling. It's loaded with slimy cinephile moments, but the story is as old as they come (A troubled, eccentric genius that chooses his progressive vision above literarily anything else) and doesn't do anything new, even the the fake cue marks are already widely used in modern cinema te emulate that black and white feeling. I've yet to see 'À bout de souffle', but I doubt 'Nouvelle Vague' will be a better artefact about the making of it, than the film itself.
