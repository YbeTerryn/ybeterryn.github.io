Babylon

Babylon is thrilling and exuberant. It's a chaotic mess, sure, but it is a funny chaotic mess. Decadence at it's most primal. Margot Robbie is fantastically riding the line between crazy attractiveness and batshit craziness. Damien Chazelle's three hour epic tale of Hollywood debauchery isn't only carried by her performance though. The rest of the cast is equally enjoyable, Brad Pitt plays himself yet another time and gets away with it, yet another time. The overall style is enjoyably over the top with some amazing set pieces throughout.
It's a love letter to film and a hate mail to the industry that makes it. Though filled with dream-like sequence full of well-choreographed excitement, doom lurks over the shoulders of every scene, perfectly creating that feeling of knowing you shouldn't enjoy it as much as you do.
Babylon's only downside is it pretentiousness that breaks some of it's impact during longer pieces of dialogue.
Even though one can see why it failed at box office, it really didn't deserve to.
