I never expected the adaption of Naked Lunch to be one of the tamer Cronenberg features. While William S. Burroughs' Naked Lunch is far form my favorite novel, I was interested in how one would translate it to the big screen. Apparently it's by provoding the vile writings with a somewhat linear narrative. Sure Cronenberg stays true to the illusive nature of the book, but it almost feels like something completely different. It has an actual structure that is not at all hard to follow. Naked Lunch is a more reserved, mature Cronenberg, where he's not just spitting out his own psychosexual fantasies purely because, but tries to encompass every scene into some bigger metaphor. Should be a recipe for a magnum opus, but Naked Lunch underdelivers it's many themes.
