I wanted to love many things about Yorgos Lanthimos latest collaboration with Jesse Plemons and Emma Stone. The main trio play their asses off, and all the social commentary is definitely there. The cinematography is interesting as always. Yet it felt like his weakest effort to this day. The pacing is not on point. The first act actually drags like hell, even if it features weird face masks and amateur kidnapping. Most interludes of Teddy and Don always seem to take longer than needed. There's something off with Bugonia. Even Lanthimos sounddesign which I have always liked got somewhat on my nerves. It was almost a parody of himself. When third act really kicks in, finally his genius shines true again. It's fast paced sequences, spiral into chaos like only the master can do it, but then the twist comes. Firstly, I predicted it, partly because I knew it was Lanthimos, but still, way to early in the film. Secondly and more importantly it's so badly executed. I'm not talking about the twist itself, that's ok, I'm talkin about that ridiculous Andromedans meeting. Whenever extra-terrestrials are shown in movies, most of the time they look so dumb and Bugonia is weirdly not an exception. Coming from a man as competent in creating weirdness as Lanthimos, it surprised me, just how stupid that scene actually looks. I've seen better Alien scenes on kids TV. And it's such a shame since the last scene, Yorgos is right back at you with his genius. It's uncanny how good the montage to 'Where Have All the Flowers Gone' is. All in all Bugonia is an interesting effort by the Great Greek Master, but it also is more flawed than I'm used to. Ps. The Flat Earth is a nice detail.
