Thanks to the listeners of my local classical radio station voting this as the greatest soundtrack of all time, I was able to go to a special screening and watch this one on the big screen for the first time. The film takes a lot of time to unfold and the second time watching I liked that even more. The first twenty minutes of just the time killing bandits is pure genius. The rest of the film is that too. The pacing is otherworldly. Even though moving slower than an old snail, one is constantly glued to the screen. Tension-building at it's finest. All the cowboy interplay, a dying breed against the backdrop of rising industry , the performance of the four main characters, all of it make it a real challenger to 'The Good The Bad and the Ugly as one of my favorites ever. My only problem lies within the ending, especially the last scene with Cheyenne feels unnecessary and breaks the flow from 'Harmonica' leaving and Jill greeting the workers. It is a small problem though as this western is almost three hours of magnificence.
