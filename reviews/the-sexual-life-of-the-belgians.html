La Vie sexuelle des Belges 1950-1978, is a flawed, almost childish endeavor, but one that could make me smile at various points. Jan Ducquoy's deadpan delivery, makes the whole thing work. Whether it's the aunt pissing right there where she stands or Herman Brusselmans scolding someone in butchered French or a dadaist shouting incomprehensible vowels in a typical Belgian brown pub. The films has a certain edge to it. In an obvious attempt to exploit and shock it's viewers, this horny biography makes a few mistakes, but let that aspect not distract you from the very real commentary on Belgian domestic life. It's essentially an evocation of a narrow-minded country family and it's product overcompensating it's freedom. Jan Ducqoy dares to look Belgians, philosophies and norms in the eye, but most of all he holds a mirror to himself. A bumbling loser renting a little room in Anderlecht, devouring women after women, while imagining at least half of them. He let's various characters belittle himself at variuous points and not without good reason. La Vie sexuelle des Belges 1950-1978 should not be simply thrown away as a dumb, exploitative movie by one of Belgium's most infamous anarchists.
