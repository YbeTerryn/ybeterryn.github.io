Szamanka

Szamanka is like a perverted person's nightmares. Even though the overall themes are actually there, they are too disjointed to mean or profoundly question anything.

Summary of the film:
Suddenly the mafia is there, suddenly it's also about radioactivity, and suicide, and marital problems, and women are sex objects, but wait, actually not and they get raped, but they like it, but just kidding, they most certainly do not and they can do the fucking too and they are insane from the get-go, but also because their abused, revenge is sweet and man for more than three thousand years have fallen victim of crazed women, but they deserve it because it's their own fault and love is better than lust, but lust gets chosen over love, not only by man, but also by women and it's above else just one big mess.

Praise of Szamanka should largely be preserved for Iwona Petry, embodying unpredictability like no one else has ever done before. Żuławski on the other hand constructs a very powerful ending, which truly saves the film, but that doesn't take away that he wasted so many time going from one primal sex scene to the other. It could really do with fewer scenes of the same thing. In the end Szamanka's biggest crime might be making primitive, sweaty, arousing sex so boringly predictable.
