One of many people's favorite films of all time, I was bound to check out Bob fosse's 'All that Jazz' at sometime. For a film so widely accepted as one of the all time greats, it really is flawed. The first half hour is not easy to get into. The acting is somewhat off and the structure has yet to take full form. But what it lacks in the first half is completely undone by the phenomenal second half. The musical numbers are excellent and serve the film in the finest way possible. The rendition of Simon and Garfunkel's 'Bye Bye Love' is one of the greatest closers a film has ever received. In the end All That Jazz does what any good biopic needs to do. Tell a comprehensible story and create empathy for the titular character, while still leaving enough mystery for wondering.
