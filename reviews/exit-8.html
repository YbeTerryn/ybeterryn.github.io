Exit 8 is a tour de force of camera movement. It's uncanny how well the camera flows in this minimalist horror drama. The suspense is there from almost the first minute. The sound design in this film is also on another level. And those things needed to be top-notch, since narrative-wise the film lacks decisiveness. For a horrorfilm all about the eeriness of the unknown, it overexplains itself a lot. Whether it's the main character spouting his thoughts out loud, while roaming alone, or the on the nose beach sequence that over explains the films metaphor. That scene is actually the worst in this otherwise entertaining, thrilling experience. The horror is kept to a minimum, but it is what works. Many reviews speak of an anti-abortion, pro-life standpoint, but I think those reviewers overgeneralize American politics. The film is not in any way anti-abortion. It is about a young man and his fear of becoming a father. Sure, that is a cheesy narrative and it's not one of the films strongpoints, but it's weakness doesn't come from an overtly political stance. Even with this personal growth-plot incorporated, which it did not really need, Exit 8 might generally and unironically be the best game adaptation I've ever seen, although that's mostly due to the competition.
