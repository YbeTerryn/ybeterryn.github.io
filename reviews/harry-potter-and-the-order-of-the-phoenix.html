Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix is a weird one to rate. There are many things to like. The battle sequences finally reach their full potential in terms of enjoyment and scale. Ralph Fiennes keeps his performance high. Voldemort is on screen for only a limited amount of time, but is again the very highpoint of the film. The overall plot really starts to thick and keeps one interested. It's funny how the school needed to become a totalitarian dictatorship, before the children actually started to enrich each other. But it's nice that the things we saw them practicing were actually useful. The main issue with Order of the Phoenix is Harry Potter himself. I get the whole battle between the good and the dark side inside him, but Harry is so fucking annoying the whole 2 hours. His pubescent behavior resembles nothing of the dark lord, but more of a silly twelve year old not getting his favorite cookie. It's like if the ring (the greatest form of power/evil in Mordor) would turn Frodo into a whiny teenager. On top of that, it's not very well acted either. Almost the whole cast of children have grown into their respective roles and Daniel Radcliffe is the only one still behind. Some plotholes ruin some of the fun again. How the fuck are they flying on thestrals they cannot see? I get that those who have seen death could guide them, but still, I feel like a two-minute scene in which they help the other on the creatures, wasn't to much to ask. But the very thing that stumps all stupidity is Dumbledore's excuse for not acknowledging Potter's struggle. Like that was the straight up dumbest shit I've heard someone say in the series, and it's not because of a lack of competition. Furthermore, it breaks his character completely.
